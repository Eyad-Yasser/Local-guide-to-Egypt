Book Review Page

  • 1984 by george Orwell

    Overview

    Set in a dystopian future, "1984" follows Winston Smith, a low-ranking member of the Party in totalitarian Oceania. Constantly surveilled by Big Brother, Winston grapples with oppression and control. His desire for individuality leads him to rebellion, but he ultimately faces the brutal reality of the regime's power and manipulation

  • To kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

    Overview

    Set in the racially charged American South during the 1930s, "To Kill a Mockingbird" follows young Scout Finch and her brother Jem as they navigate childhood and moral complexities. Their father, Atticus, defends a Black man accused of raping a white woman, exposing them to prejudice and injustice while fostering empathy.

  • The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald

    Overview

    "The Great Gatsby" captures the opulence and disillusionment of the Roaring Twenties through the eyes of Nick Carraway. He befriends the enigmatic Jay Gatsby, who is obsessed with rekindling a romance with Daisy Buchanan. The novel explores themes of love, ambition, and the moral decay hidden beneath the era's glamour.
